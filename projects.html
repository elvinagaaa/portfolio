Projects

Project 1: Beijing Clean Air Quality Analysis

This project analyzes air pollution data in Beijing, focusing on the levels of PM10 and PM2.5 particles. The analysis explores the trends and seasonal patterns in air quality and identifies factors influencing pollution levels. Key variables considered include SO2, NO2, CO, O3, dew point, wind speed, and rainfall. The study finds a significant decreasing trend in PM10 levels and a less pronounced decline in PM2.5 levels. It also highlights that wind speed has a substantial impact on air pollution, while increased rainfall tends to lower pollutant concentrations. The results provide insights into the temporal patterns and contributing factors of air quality in the region.

Project 2: Bike Sharing Analysis

This project involves analyzing bicycle rental data, focusing on factors that influence rental rates under different weather conditions. The analysis includes examining the impact of variables such as temperature, humidity, and weather conditions on bike rentals. Key insights include identifying user preferences in terms of weather and environmental conditions, such as increased rentals during specific humidity levels. The results help in understanding user behavior and optimizing bike-sharing services based on weather patterns.

Project 3: Perencanaan Strategis Sistem Informasi Manajemen Coffee D-19 Ambarawa

The project titled "Laporan Akhir Perencanaan Strategis Sistem Informasi Manajemen Menggunakan Metode Ward and Peppard" for Coffee D-19 in Ambarawa involves developing a comprehensive information systems strategy. The aim is to align the IT and IS strategies with the overall business objectives of Coffee D-19, enhancing its efficiency, effectiveness, and competitiveness. The project proposes several information systems applications, including SI Procurement, SI Reservasi, SI Keuangan, SI Order, SI Kepegawaian, SI Membership, SI Barang, and a website. These systems cover various aspects of business operations, from inventory management and reservations to financial accounting and customer relationship management. The strategic plan also includes a proposed IT infrastructure, utilizing a LAN-based network topology to support the systems. The project emphasizes the implementation of these systems and the supporting hardware, highlighting their potential to streamline operations and support business growth. The McFarlan Strategic Grid is used to categorize the systems and hardware based on their operational importance and potential for future development. The plan outlines a phased implementation approach, prioritizing key operational systems for 2024 and strategic systems for 2025.

Project 4: Manajemen Proyek Singkong Keju D-9 Salatiga

The project titled "Studi Kasus 'Singkong Keju D-9 Salatiga'" focuses on implementing an integrated Point of Sales (POS) system for the company Singkong Keju D-9 in Salatiga. The primary goal is to transition from a manual recording system to a computerized solution that enhances efficiency and accuracy in financial reporting, inventory management, and transaction processing. The project involves the design and implementation of a system that automates sales transactions, updates product stock in real-time, and generates financial reports. The project also includes detailed planning, risk management, and documentation efforts to ensure a successful implementation. The Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) outlines all the phases, from project initiation to closing, including specific tasks and responsibilities. This comprehensive approach aims to minimize human error, improve data accuracy, and enhance operational efficiency for Singkong Keju D-9.

Project 5: Design Arsitektur Enterprise Bloods Salatiga

My project, "Analisis Proses Bisnis pada Bloods Store Salatiga," is a detailed case study aimed at improving the business processes of Bloods Store, a fashion retail company in Salatiga. As a student in the Information Systems Program at Universitas Kristen Satya Wacana, I am conducting this study under the guidance of Anthony, S.SI., M.Cs. The purpose of this project is to analyze the existing business processes and identify opportunities for improvement through the application of enterprise architecture frameworks. This involves a series of interviews to understand the store's vision, mission, organizational structure, and process flows. Through this analysis, I am examining the store's business, information systems, and technology architecture to identify gaps and provide actionable recommendations. The ultimate goal is to enhance the store's operational efficiency and effectiveness by leveraging modern technological solutions. This project not only enhances my understanding of business process analysis and enterprise architecture but also contributes to the real-world improvement of a local business, aligning with the university's mission of combining education, research, and community service.
